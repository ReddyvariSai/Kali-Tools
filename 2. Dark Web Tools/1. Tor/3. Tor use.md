## How to Update Tor Browser

Keeping Tor Browser updated ensures that you are protected from known vulnerabilities and benefit from the latest security features. 

Updates are essential to maintaining anonymity online.

To ensure you stay protected, always use the latest version of Tor Browser. Here’s a simple way to keep it updated:

#### 1. Automatic Updates via Launcher:

```
torbrowser-launcher --update
```
#### 2. If You Installed Manually:

Download the latest version from Tor Project — https://www.torproject.org/, verify the GPG signature, extract the files, and replace the existing installation.

Extract the updated files and replace your existing Tor Browser folder with the new version.

#### 3. Check for Updates Within the Browser:

If Tor Browser is already open, you can check for updates by:

Clicking the menu button (three horizontal lines in the top-right corner) >

Navigating to Help > About Tor Browser.

Tor Browser will automatically check for updates and prompt you to download if needed.

#### Using a VPN with Tor:

While Tor Browser enhances your privacy, pairing it with a VPN can significantly boost your anonymity. Here’s how they work together and why it’s beneficial:

1. What Does a VPN Do?

A VPN encrypts your internet traffic and hides your IP address by routing your connection through its secure servers. This means your Internet Service Provider (ISP) cannot see that you’re using Tor or track your online activity.

2. How Tor and VPNs Work Together:

VPN Before Tor (Recommended): Your internet traffic is encrypted by the VPN first and then sent to the Tor network. This ensures that your ISP cannot detect you’re using Tor.

Tor Before VPN: Less common and more complex, this routes your Tor traffic through a VPN after leaving the Tor network. It may provide anonymity from the VPN itself but is harder to set up and less secure for most users.

#### 1. Choosing a Reliable VPN:

Select a VPN with the following features:

> * No-Logs Policy: Ensure the VPN provider does not keep records of your activity.
> * High-Speed Servers: Tor can slow your connection, so a fast VPN helps mitigate this.
> * Strong Encryption: Look for AES-256 encryption and advanced security features.

#### 2. Examples of Trusted VPNs for Privacy:

> * ProtonVPN: Offers a free plan with high security and privacy standards.
> * Mullvad VPN: Focused on privacy with anonymous signup options.
> * NordVPN: A reliable choice with advanced features like Double VPN.

#### 3. Quick Setup:

> * Download and install the VPN software on your system.
> * Connect to a secure VPN server.
> * Once connected, open Tor Browser and browse safely.

#### Why Use Both?

Combining a VPN with Tor adds an extra layer of security, ensuring your ISP and other entities cannot detect Tor usage. It also protects against malicious exit nodes that could monitor unencrypted traffic.

## Stay Safe While Using Tor Browser:

While Tor Browser provides excellent privacy and anonymity, it is important to remember that improper usage can still expose your identity.

Follow these best practices to ensure a secure experience:

1. Always Use HTTPS:

Ensure the websites you visit are secured with HTTPS. Sites using HTTP can expose your activity to attackers, even when using Tor. Install a browser extension like HTTPS Everywhere to enforce secure connections.

2. Avoid Downloading Files or Plugins:

Do not download files, plugins, or additional software while using Tor. Downloaded content may bypass Tor’s protection and leak your IP address or personal information.

3. Do Not Use Personal Accounts:

Refrain from logging into personal accounts like email or social media. Doing so could reveal your identity, as these services often track user activity.

4. Disable JavaScript (if necessary):

Some malicious websites exploit JavaScript vulnerabilities. While Tor tries to mitigate this, you can manually disable JavaScript from Tor Browser’s settings for enhanced security (but note that it may break website functionality).

5. Be Cautious of Exit Nodes:

Traffic exiting the Tor network can still be monitored. Avoid entering sensitive information on unencrypted websites.

6. Avoid clicking on unverified .onion links or interacting with sites you don’t trust, as they could host malicious content or compromise your privacy.
